CSC 111 Data Structures and Abstractions

Assignments

Our assignments fall into two categories:

  1. Autograded: these assignments are graded by a set of automated tests. A task must pass all of its tests to count as complete, and any requirements being tested are either stated in the assignment or revealed in the feedback after you pass certain tests. They cover most of the topics in our course objectives and make up the bulk of your grade.
  2. Other: there will be at least one larger open-ended assignment, and there may be other small assignments or optional tasks that will earn you a few extra points.

Feedback

You will receive a link to a shared OneDrive folder near the beginning of the semester, which you should star or bookmark. I will upload all assignment feedback to that folder. Expect feedback on autograded assignments by the end of the day every Monday, Wednesday, and Friday.

Most feedback I give will be automatically generated by my tests. You should review this feedback yourself, but feel free to ask me if you have questions about it. I automate parts of the grading process so that I can handle large numbers of submissions and revisions, not because I don’t want to discuss your code with you. The automation gives me more time to work with people who have questions or need extra help. It also allows for faster feedback and more opportunities for you to revise work.

Deadlines

This covers what’s expected of both you and me with regards to deadlines. It’s on the long side, but I want to make these expectations clear.

You should expect the following from me:

  • Deadlines are end of day (11:59 PM ET) on the given date unless stated otherwise.
  • Deadlines are strict; late work isn’t accepted no matter how close it is to the deadline.
  • Deadlines allow at least two more weeks than an assignment is expected to take.
  • New assignments and changes to current assignments are announced on Blackboard.
  • Autograded assignments are graded at least three times per week, typically Mondays, Wednesdays, and Fridays. I will only grade the most recent submission for each assignment.
  • If I make a mistake or fail to meet an obligation in a way that negatively affects students, then I will extend deadlines or modify assignments to minimize the impact of my mistakes.

I will expect the following from you:

  • Start each assignment the week it is announced.
  • Test programs before submitting them and follow directions for submitting work.
  • Expect unexpected problems and leave yourself enough time to deal with them.
  • Autograded assignments:
    • Finish and submit the first task the week the assignment is announced.
    • Don’t wait until a task passes all of its tests before starting work on another task. Most tasks can be partially or fully completed without completing every prior task.
    • Check feedback regularly and submit revisions within a few days of receiving feedback.
    • Resubmit an assignment whenever you fix an incomplete task or finish a new task.
  • Other assignments: aim to finish each task at least a week before its deadline.

If you follow all of this advice and still have trouble meeting a deadline, then see the section below about Extensions.

Invalid Submissions

There are several reasons that all or part of a submission can’t be processed or graded by my scripts, including:

  • Submitting the wrong files or submitting files in the wrong format
  • Naming files incorrectly
  • Submitting code that doesn’t compile because you forgot to run it first
  • Ignoring a restriction in the assignment, such as not using part of the standard library
  • Not following directions when submitting

Usually this will result in the whole submission or affected tasks being marked as incomplete with minimal feedback, and you will need to correct the mistakes in your next submission. If a submission is invalid, that does not entitle you to extra time or extra submissions.

Blackboard

All assignments must be submitted on Blackboard, and all files must be attached to the submission. I will generally not accept emailed submissions or links to files on OneDrive, Google Drive, etc.

Blackboard will usually not show which submissions were graded or what grade was received on a particular submission. That information can be found in the Feedback folder on OneDrive. Blackboard will show your current grade on each assignment and your overall grade in the class.

Extensions

In rare situations I will give individual students extra time to complete an assignment. These circumstances typically fall into two categories:

  1. You have an accommodation approved by the school that entitles you to an extension. This will be handled according to the terms of the accommodation, and you must notify me promptly when you need to use your accommodation.
  2. You can demonstrate that you’ve met the expectations in the Deadlines section and contact me promptly about the need for an extension. Your submission record should show early and frequent submissions with clear attempts to address issues shown in the feedback.
    • If you’re dealing with an emergency or a situation where it will be difficult to engage with coursework for an extented period of time, then the extension will depend on how early you contact me and how long the issue affects your ability to work on assignments.
    • If you need more time because you’re struggling with the assignment, then I expect you to seek extra help to better understand the relevant topics. For example, you can see a peer tutor at the Learning Commons or stop by my office hours.
